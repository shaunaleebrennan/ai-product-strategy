**Product:**

## Scores

### Contextual Moat — 3/5
*Workflow depth × switching cost. Would users leave in a weekend if a competitor showed up?*

**Score rationale: Comms teams build their whole content workflow inside Workvivo — drafting, publishing, distributing. That creates real habit. But the actual writing part? Intercal communicators already use ChatGPT for that. The moat is the distribution, a single unified experience layer, and engagement data to support stratgey, not the creation itself.**

**Named attacker: Microsoft Copilot**

---

### Data Advantage — 4/5
*Proprietary signal that compounds with usage. What do you see that OpenAI doesn't?*

**Score rationale: Workvivo knows what comms actually land — which messages get read, which get ignored, which drive action. That feedback loop makes the AI comms agent smarter over time in a way no general-purpose AI can replicate. OpenAI has no idea how your employees respond to internal communications, and it can only guide the content based on the information that's given which can legnthen time to value, and leave context gaps.**

**Named attacker: OpenAI**

---

### Platform Exposure — 2/5
*Encroachment risk × pivot speed. If Apple/Google/OpenAI ships your hero feature native — then what?*

**Score rationale: AI writing tools are everywhere and getting better fast. The multi-format angle (turn one post into a video, podcast, email) is genuinely differentiated right now — but it won't be for long. Unily already has it. This is the most urgent risk.**

**Named attacker: Unily (already planning to ship this — observed its roadmap at the Digital Workplace Summit)**

## Top Vulnerability
<!-- One line: what's the single biggest strategic risk? -->
Unily is already shipping multi-format content today, and if OpenAI makes writing tools free inside enterprise workflows, the AI creation layer becomes a commodity and Workvivo's edge shrinks to just being the place content lives.

Why not High:
