## Board Pitch

**Thesis (1 sentence):** Workvivo HQ is the AI-native headquarters for every employee — three product pillars on one platform, fed by an expanding connector ecosystem (major enterprise systems at launch, 1000+ in H2) plus user-generated content competitors structurally can't see, priced at $6 combined to reach the 80% of the workforce Microsoft and Glean can't.

**The case:** HQ delivers the only enterprise AI experience that combines connected-system knowledge with how people actually feel and what they engage with. Communication & Engagement, Search & Knowledge, and People Intelligence pillars all feed the same platform signal. Microsoft Copilot sees email and meetings. Glean indexes work product. Unily sees published content. We see all three sources fed into three pillars that share signal, reaching 9M employees with 90% adoption and 96% retention — the proof points no new entrant can replicate. Every AI feature across all three pillars ships at the June 14 launch, including Seer Standalone as a new commercial motion targeting the Glint replacement market. This isn't a phased roadmap, it's a complete category launch — with the connector ecosystem expanding in H2 to deepen the moat.

**Why now:** Microsoft Copilot Cowork is shipping in Frontier preview today, with GA in 6–12 months. We have a closing window to define the EX-AI category before Microsoft consolidates it into the M365 stack via partner-built EX skills. June 14 HQ Core launch is the moment — and Agentic Search is demo-ready today, meaning we go head-to-head with Glean and Copilot from day one across the major enterprise systems customers care about most. The full 1000-connector ecosystem rolls out in H2 alongside HQ+ Enterprise / ZoomMate full agentic execution, extending the moat through the window Microsoft would otherwise close on us.

**What's defensible:** Cross-Domain Transfer is the only active compounding loop and the structural moat — three pillars sharing signal that competitors structurally can't see. Plus Level 3 governance posture (zero data retention with model providers, no training on customer content, EU residency via AWS Bedrock) that most enterprise AI tools can't claim. Plus frontline reach (80% of the workforce that Copilot and Glean don't license). The H2 expansion of the connector ecosystem to 1000+ deepens the moat further — it's structurally where the agentic orchestration layer turns from "search across enterprise systems" into "execute work across enterprise systems," which Microsoft's Copilot can replicate but only inside the M365 boundary.

**The economics:** $4 platform + $2 HQ AI = $6 combined PUPM, with HQ+ at $10 and HQ+ Enterprise (ZoomMate) at $24. Combined gross margin ~50% blended at list. AI tier alone is ~6% — deliberately thin to drive adoption. From day one, HQ at $2 AI tier delivers Agentic Search across the major enterprise systems plus the full Communication & Engagement and People Intelligence AI surfaces — the same job Glean charges $45 and Copilot $30 for, available to the full workforce instead of just the desk license holder. At enterprise scale (~410K HQ users), combined contract approaches $30M annually with $9.8M of that as AI tier ARR. Seer Standalone adds a parallel commercial motion at HQ pricing without requiring a full Workvivo platform deal. 15x cheaper than Copilot, 22x cheaper than Glean on AI alone.

**The risks:** Loop 1 broken — Recursive Learning captures signals but doesn't compound them yet (flagged across M2, M4, M5 as one problem in three modules). Loop 1 closure is on the H3 roadmap, but our H2 bets all depend on it working sooner. Microsoft extending Cowork with EX skills via the M365 App Store inside 12 months. Internal Zoom absorption if HQ becomes the most valuable launch in the portfolio. AI tier near break-even at $2 entry pricing means we're exposed to cost shocks until the cross-pillar orchestration ships. Connector ecosystem expansion in H2 has execution risk — getting from launch-set to 1000+ in 6-9 months requires sustained engineering investment alongside Zoom co-build for the agentic layer.

**Trust / failure modes:** Confidence UX shows scores but reasoning depth is thin — addressed by Loop 1 fix. Hallucination defended on sensitive content via refusal rules and Tier 3 review, but the in-between zone (uncertain but not clearly sensitive) is the weakest layer today. Air Canada precedent applies — every AI commitment is a company commitment, which is why HQ+ Enterprise agentic execution requires human approval at every system-of-record write.

**Scale / governance:** Level 3 Trust Architecture — zero retention with third-party model providers (Anthropic, OpenAI via Zoom), no training on customer content (published commitment), EU residency via AWS Bedrock for European customers. Tiered HITL with role-based escalation. Audit cadence: weekly automated eval, monthly 200-draft sample, quarterly policy review, annual external review. Owners named at every cadence.

**Competitive:** Microsoft Copilot Cowork (primary, $30/user, desk-only, agentic but for productivity not EX). Glean (adjacent, $45/user, search-only, no sentiment layer). Unily (vertical, comms-built, no platform signal density). Workvivo HQ: $6 combined, every-employee, three pillars feeding each other, 3-4x higher adoption than Viva. From June 14, Agentic Search across major enterprise connectors plus the full Communication & Engagement and People Intelligence AI surfaces at $2 AI tier directly competes with Glean ($45) and Copilot ($30) — not as a roadmap promise, as a shipping product. The 1000+ connector ecosystem rolling out in H2 extends our agentic reach into territory Microsoft's Copilot Cowork can't follow without breaking out of the M365 boundary. Microsoft has 78 things named Copilot. We have one HQ.

**The ask:** Accelerate Loop 1 closure from H3 into H2. The roadmap shows Loop 1 fully closed as a 9–18 month outcome, which is the right ambition — but our H2 bets all depend on it working sooner. HQ+ Enterprise / ZoomMate full agentic execution (with the 1000-connector expansion) only compounds if signals from each agentic action feed back into the model. Automated actions in Seer only get more accurate over time if corrections feed the learning loop. Network Intelligence pilot only delivers cross-customer benchmarks worth deploying if Loop 1 is already creating cleaner signal at the customer level. Without parallel Loop 1 investment alongside H2, those features ship as point capabilities rather than as compounding intelligence — which means Microsoft can close the gap with feature parity rather than having to rebuild governance architecture. 6-month engineering investment, pulled into H2, with a clear progress metric (override rate on medium-confidence predictions declining quarter-over-quarter) and a hard outcome (AI tier blended margin trending from ~6% toward 40%+ over 18–24 months).

## M1 Baseline vs. Now

**M1 baseline (3-sentence AI strategy at the start of the course):**

> Internal communicators will adopt Workvivo's AI Comms Agent (Liquid Content) not because it writes better content, but because it's the only tool that tells them whether their content will actually land, before they hit publish. The bet is on engagement-prediction as the unique signal Workvivo sees that no general-purpose AI does. Adoption among comms managers drives the wedge into the broader Workvivo AI proposition.

**Now (after Modules 1–5):**

> Workvivo HQ is the AI-native headquarters for every employee — three product pillars (Communication & Engagement, Search & Knowledge, People Intelligence) on one platform, fed by an expanding connector ecosystem (major enterprise systems at launch, 1000+ in H2) and user-generated content competitors structurally can't see. The bet is reach plus signal density: priced at $2–6 to cover the 80% of the workforce Microsoft Copilot ($30) and Glean ($45) structurally can't, while the three pillars share signal in a way no point tool can replicate. The Loop 1 fix — closing the recursive learning loop — is the single highest-leverage investment that turns the moat from "active today" into "compounding for the long arc."

**What shifted:**

*From feature to category.* M1 bet was on a single AI feature (multi-format comms generation). The category bet is HQ as an AI-native EX platform. Same underlying product DNA, an order-of-magnitude larger strategic claim.

*Liquid Content moved from headline to H3 bet.* The most interesting shift: what M1 called the wedge feature isn't even in the launch. Liquid Content — the campaign orchestrator that drove the original product strategy work — is now a 9–18 month bet, sitting in H3 alongside Presentations Builder, Zoom AI Docs integration, and Connected intelligence from HRIS/L&D. The reason is the platform vision turned out to be bigger than the original feature. Reach (expanding connector ecosystem, frontline workforce coverage) and signal density (three-pillar Cross-Domain Transfer) moved ahead of multi-format content generation in the priority order. Liquid Content stays in the roadmap — but as the next-level campaign orchestration tier, not the launch hero.

*From one buyer to every employee.* M1 bet was on the comms manager as primary user. The HQ bet is workforce-wide. The pricing structure ($2–6 per user, designed for the 80/20 split) follows from that shift.

*New commercial motion: Seer Standalone.* Not anticipated at M1. Seer Standalone launches at June 14 as the Glint replacement, selling without requiring a full Workvivo platform deal. This is a meaningful new SKU and GTM motion — not in the M1 framing because M1 was anchored to a single AI feature inside the existing Workvivo platform.

*From one moat to two stacked moats.* M1 named engagement prediction as the unique signal. M5 surfaced the deeper moat: three-pillar Cross-Domain Transfer plus Level 3 governance. Engagement prediction is still real — it's just one expression of a wider signal density, and it's the metric the H3 Liquid Content bet hinges on.

*From "we'll build it" to "we built it, and here's what's broken — and here's the plan to fix it on the roadmap."* M1 confidence was Medium because the data story hadn't been told loudly enough. Current confidence is also Medium, but for a sharper reason: Loop 1 is broken across three modules, we know it, and Loop 1 closure is now explicitly on the H3 roadmap as a named outcome with measurable targets. That self-awareness — naming the gap and showing the plan to close it on a defined timeline — is what makes the bet credible.

*From phased rollout to category launch with H2 expansion.* M1 implied gradual feature rollout. The reality is all AI features across all three pillars ship at the June 14 launch as one category move, with the connector ecosystem expanding in H2 (from launch-set to 1000+) and HQ+ Enterprise / ZoomMate full agentic execution arriving in the 3–9 month window. The phasing is now commercial (HQ tier adoption first, HQ+ upsell later, HQ+ Enterprise on request), connector-ecosystem (initial set at launch, 1000+ in H2), and roadmap-bet (Liquid Content, Presentations, Zoom Docs, Connected HRIS/L&D in H3) — rather than product-development phasing of the core AI features.

*From "will it work" to "we have H2 bets that depend on Loop 1."* M1 didn't have downstream dependencies because the strategy was a single feature. The HQ roadmap has H2 bets (HQ+ Enterprise full agentic with 1000-connector expansion, Automated Seer actions, Network Intelligence) that all depend on Loop 1 working. That's why the Board Ask is to accelerate Loop 1 into H2 — without it, the H2 product investment ships as capability without compounding.
