## The Case (why we should invest)

Workvivo HQ is the only AI experience for employees that brings together three things on one platform:

1. **Knowledge from your connected systems** — Microsoft 365, ServiceNow, Workday, Google.
2. **How people actually feel** — sentiment, engagement, what's working and what isn't.
3. **What people actually do** — what content they react to, what gets ignored.

Microsoft Copilot only sees email and meetings. Glean only sees documents. Unily only sees published content. **Workvivo HQ sees all three — and reaches 9 million employees who already use Workvivo every day. 90% of them open it. 96% of them stay.**

Every AI feature ships on June 14. Including Seer Standalone, which goes head-to-head with Glint as a new product we can sell on its own. This isn't a phased rollout. It's a complete launch.

**Why now:** Microsoft is shipping its own version of this — Copilot Cowork is in preview today, generally available in 6 to 12 months. We have a closing window to claim the category before Microsoft does.

**What makes us hard to copy:**

- The three pillars share signal. When someone reads a post (Comms), searches for help (Search), and shows low engagement (Seer), the AI sees all three at once. A point tool can't.
- Our governance posture: we never train AI on customer content, we don't let model providers keep customer data, and we store EU data in Europe. Most enterprise AI tools can't say all three.
- We reach the 80% of the workforce that Copilot and Glean don't license.

## The Model (does the math hold)

- **Pricing:** $4 for the Workvivo platform + $2 for HQ AI = **$6 per user per month**. HQ+ at $10. HQ+ Enterprise (the full agentic version, ZoomMate, available on request only) at $24.
- **Profit margin:** About 50% on average across the bundle. The AI piece alone runs at about 6% margin — we made it deliberately thin to drive adoption.
- **At enterprise scale (around 410,000 HQ users):** A single contract approaches **$30M a year**, with $9.8M of that coming from the AI add-on. Seer Standalone gives us a second sales motion we can run independently.
- **Versus the competition on AI alone:** We're **15 times cheaper than Copilot. 22 times cheaper than Glean.** Same work — but available to the entire workforce, not just the desk holder.

---

## The Risks (what could go wrong)

- **Our learning loop is broken.** Right now we capture every correction users make, but the system doesn't actually get smarter from those corrections yet. It's the same problem showing up in three modules — data, reliability, governance. The fix is on the H3 roadmap.
- **Microsoft extends Copilot into employee experience inside 12 months.** Likely via partner-built skills in their M365 App Store. We respond by shipping our category-defining AI now and hardening the cross-pillar story in H2.
- **Zoom absorbs HQ as a Zoom feature** if it becomes the most valuable launch in the portfolio. Seer Standalone protects us — it gives Workvivo a sales motion that doesn't depend on Zoom.
- **The AI add-on is near break-even at $2.** We're exposed if AI costs go up. The fix: route most queries to a cheaper, smaller AI model and only use the expensive one for complex work.
- **Trust:** Our confidence indicators are too thin right now — the learning loop fix solves this. And the Air Canada precedent matters: when a company's AI chatbot promised a fake refund policy, the airline had to honor it. Every AI commitment is a company commitment. That's why our most powerful AI features always require a human to approve before anything is saved or sent.
- **Six things the board will ask about:**
  - How often does the AI make stuff up?
  - How fast does accuracy decay if we leave it alone?
  - Is the AI confident when it should be, and uncertain when it should be?
  - How often does a human have to review the output?
  - How much revenue do we make per dollar we spend on AI?
  - When we update the model, does quality get better or worse?

All six are tracked in our Reliability Contract.

## The Ask

**Deprioritise Liquid Content as a bundled feature. Move it to premium-only.**

This is the single biggest learning from the work. Liquid Content — the multi-format AI content engine — was always going to be the headline feature of the AI strategy. But the unit economics don't work at $2 per user per month.

Every Liquid Content generation carries real cost: image generation, video, audio, podcast formatting — each one is expensive AI inference. At the $2 HQ AI tier, three or four uses per user per month wipes the margin. If we bundle Liquid Content into the base AI tier, we're either subsidising power users at the expense of the rest, or we're capping usage in a way that makes the feature feel broken.

**What I'm asking for:**

1. **Liquid Content does not ship in the $2 HQ AI bundle.** Period.
2. **Liquid Content moves to HQ+ Enterprise as a premium upsell only** — where the $24 per user pricing can absorb the inference cost, and where customers who need multi-format generation at scale can pay for what they're actually consuming.
3. **Optionally, offer Liquid Content as a usage-based add-on** to HQ+ customers — so we capture demand from the middle of the market without putting pressure on margin.
4. **Rebuild Liquid Content v2 in H3** with the right commercial frame from day one: premium tier feature, usage-based pricing, scoped to the 2-3 content formats that actually move engagement.

**Why this is the right call:**

- It protects the launch economics. We can ship HQ AI at $2 with confidence in the margin.
- It creates a clean upsell story for sales. "If you need multi-format content at scale, that's HQ+ Enterprise."
- It's commercially honest. We're not pretending the math works when it doesn't.
- It doesn't kill the feature. It just puts it in the tier where the price can pay for the cost.

This is the kind of decision a strategy is supposed to surface — and the kind that's hard to make once a feature has been positioned as a headline.

### Horizon 2 — Validate (1–3 months)

| Initiative | Strategy Component | Hypothesis | Kill Criteria | Confidence |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Expand connector ecosystem (toward 1000+) | Moat | Every new connector makes Search more useful and the moat wider | If new connectors aren't lifting Search usage or retention by week 8, we cut the long tail and focus on the top 50 | M |
| Seer recommends actions automatically (with human review) | Bet | People leaders will accept AI-recommended actions if they can review, edit, or reverse them | If fewer than half of recommendations are accepted by week 8, we go back to insight-only | M |
| Smart routing v1: cheaper AI model first, expensive only when needed | Margin | We can hold AI profit margin above 65% at the $2 price point without any quality drop | If the cheaper model handles fewer than 65% of queries, or eval scores drop more than 3 points, we re-architect | M |
| Cross-customer learning pilot (10 design partners, opt-in) | Moat | Customers will share anonymous learnings if the value is concrete and the privacy contract is clear | If fewer than 4 of 10 opt in by week 10, we keep learning private to each customer | L |
| ZoomMate as a usage-based add-on ($20 + overages, request only) | Margin | The 5-8% of customers who need full agentic AI will pay the higher price, without us subsidising the rest | If fewer than 3 enterprise customers convert in 90 days, we rethink pricing or packaging | M |

- (a) The most over-loaded horizon is **H1** — five initiatives all anchored to June 14. That's unavoidable for a category launch, but it means H2 and H3 are thinner than they should be. The risk is a strong launch followed by no follow-up in months 2-6.
- (b) The H3 bet I'd protect if budget got cut is **the learning loop fix.** Every H2 bet depends on it to compound, and it's the structural defence against Microsoft catching up on features.
- (c) The one initiative I'd kill today is **Liquid Content in the base bundle.** It can't ship profitably at $2 per user per month. Moving it to premium-only protects the launch and creates a clean upsell.

## M1 → Now Reflection

**M1 baseline (three sentences I would have written at the start of the course):**

> Internal communicators will adopt Workvivo's AI Comms Agent (Liquid Content) not because it writes better content, but because it's the only tool that tells them whether their content will actually land, before they hit publish. The bet is that predicting engagement is a unique signal Workvivo can see that no general-purpose AI can. Adoption among comms managers gives us the wedge into the broader Workvivo AI proposition.

**Now (three sentences after Modules 1-5):**

> Workvivo HQ is the experience layer for work, AI, and people — three pillars on one platform, fed by an expanding library of connections to enterprise systems and millions of pieces of content competitors can't see. The bet is reach plus signal density: priced at $2-6 to cover the 80% of the workforce Microsoft Copilot ($30) and Glean ($45) structurally can't, while three pillars share signal in a way no point tool can replicate. The single most important commercial decision is keeping Liquid Content out of the base bundle — its unit economics don't work at $2, so it moves to premium-only and becomes the upsell, not the wedge.

**The single sharpest shift:**

Six weeks ago my AI strategy was a single feature — Liquid Content. Today it's a category bet against Microsoft, and the most important thing I learned was that Liquid Content can't ship in the base AI bundle at all. The math doesn't work at $2 per user per month. So Liquid Content moves out of the launch entirely and becomes a premium-only feature in HQ+ Enterprise. Same product DNA. A completely different commercial frame. **That's the course working.**
