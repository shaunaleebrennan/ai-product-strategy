## Board Pitch

**Thesis (1 sentence):** Workvivo HQ is the AI-native headquarters for every employee — three product pillars on one platform, fed by 1000+ third-party connectors plus user-generated content competitors structurally can't see, priced at $6 combined to reach the 80% of the workforce Microsoft and Glean can't.

**The case:** HQ delivers the only enterprise AI experience that combines connected-system knowledge with how people actually feel and what they engage with. Communication & Engagement, Search & Knowledge, and People Intelligence pillars all feed the same platform signal. Microsoft Copilot sees email and meetings. Glean indexes work product. Unily sees published content. We see all three sources fed into three pillars that share signal, reaching 9M employees with 90% adoption and 96% retention — the proof points no new entrant can replicate.

**Why now:** Microsoft Copilot Cowork is shipping in Frontier preview today, with GA in 6–12 months. We have a closing window to define the EX-AI category before Microsoft consolidates it into the M365 stack via partner-built EX skills. June 14 HQ Core launch is the moment, and Enterprise Search across 1000+ connected systems is demo-ready and shipping at launch — meaning we go head-to-head with Glean and Copilot from day one, not after a 9-month integration build. The next 18 months decide whether HQ becomes the category or becomes an upsell Microsoft eventually gives away for free.

**What's defensible:** Cross-Domain Transfer is the only active compounding loop and the structural moat — three pillars sharing signal that competitors structurally can't see. Plus Level 3 governance posture (zero data retention with model providers, no training on customer content, EU residency via AWS Bedrock) that most enterprise AI tools can't claim. Plus frontline reach (80% of the workforce that Copilot and Glean don't license).

**The economics:** $4 platform + $2 HQ AI = $6 combined PUPM, with HQ+ at $10 and HQ+ Enterprise (ZoomMate) at $24. Combined gross margin ~50% blended at list. AI tier alone is ~6% — deliberately thin to drive adoption. From day one, HQ at $2 AI tier delivers Enterprise Search across 1000+ connected systems — the same job Glean charges $45 and Copilot $30 for, available to the full workforce instead of just the desk license holder. At enterprise scale (~410K HQ users), combined contract approaches $30M annually with $9.8M of that as AI tier ARR. 15x cheaper than Copilot, 22x cheaper than Glean on AI alone.

**The risks:** Loop 1 broken — Recursive Learning captures signals but doesn't compound them yet (flagged across M2, M4, M5 as one problem in three modules). Microsoft extending Cowork with EX skills via the M365 App Store inside 12 months. Internal Zoom absorption if HQ becomes the most valuable launch in the portfolio. AI tier near break-even at $2 entry pricing means we're exposed to cost shocks until the cross-pillar orchestration ships.

**Trust / failure modes:** Confidence UX shows scores but reasoning depth is thin — addressed in Loop 1 fix. Hallucination defended on sensitive content via refusal rules and Tier 3 review, but the in-between zone (uncertain but not clearly sensitive) is the weakest layer today. Air Canada precedent applies — every AI commitment is a company commitment, which is why HQ+ Enterprise agentic execution requires human approval at every system-of-record write.

**Scale / governance:** Level 3 Trust Architecture — zero retention with third-party model providers (Anthropic, OpenAI via Zoom), no training on customer content (published commitment), EU residency via AWS Bedrock for European customers. Tiered HITL with role-based escalation. Audit cadence: weekly automated eval, monthly 200-draft sample, quarterly policy review, annual external review. Owners named at every cadence.

**Competitive:** Microsoft Copilot Cowork (primary, $30/user, desk-only, agentic but for productivity not EX). Glean (adjacent, $45/user, search-only, no sentiment layer). Unily (vertical, comms-built, no platform signal density). Workvivo HQ: $6 combined, every-employee, three pillars feeding each other, 3-4x higher adoption than Viva. From June 14, Enterprise Search across 1000+ connectors at $2 AI tier directly competes with Glean ($45) and Copilot ($30) — not as a roadmap promise, as a shipping product. Microsoft has 78 things named Copilot. We have one HQ.

**The ask:** Fund the cross-pillar orchestration layer that closes Loop 1 — the broken correction-learning loop flagged across M2, M4, M5. 6-month engineering investment with a clear progress metric (override rate on medium-confidence predictions declining quarter-over-quarter) and a hard outcome (AI tier blended margin trending from ~6% toward 40%+ over 18–24 months). Without this fix, the moat plateaus and AI margin stays near break-even. With it, the data flywheel finally compounds — and Microsoft can't ship the equivalent from the Copilot stack without giving up their own governance position.

## M1 Baseline vs. Now

**M1 baseline (3-sentence AI strategy at the start of the course):**

> Internal communicators will adopt Workvivo's AI Comms Agent (Liquid Content) not because it writes better content, but because it's the only tool that tells them whether their content will actually land, before they hit publish. The bet is on engagement-prediction as the unique signal Workvivo sees that no general-purpose AI does. Adoption among comms managers drives the wedge into the broader Workvivo AI proposition.

**Now (after Modules 1–5):**

> Workvivo HQ is the AI-native headquarters for every employee — three product pillars (Communication & Engagement, Search & Knowledge, People Intelligence) on one platform, fed by 1000+ third-party connectors and user-generated content competitors structurally can't see. The bet is reach plus signal density: priced at $2–6 to cover the 80% of the workforce Microsoft Copilot ($30) and Glean ($45) structurally can't, while the three pillars share signal in a way no point tool can replicate. The Loop 1 fix — closing the recursive learning loop — is the single highest-leverage investment that turns the moat from "active today" into "compounding for the long arc."

**What shifted:**

*From feature to category.* M1 bet was on a single AI feature (multi-format comms generation). The category bet is HQ as an AI-native EX platform. Same underlying product DNA, an order-of-magnitude larger strategic claim.

*From one buyer to every employee.* M1 bet was on the comms manager as primary user. The HQ bet is workforce-wide. The pricing structure ($2–6 per user, designed for the 80/20 split) follows from that shift.

*From one moat to two stacked moats.* M1 named engagement prediction as the unique signal. M5 surfaced the deeper moat: three-pillar Cross-Domain Transfer plus Level 3 governance. Engagement prediction is still real — it's just one expression of a wider signal density.

*From "we'll build it" to "we built it, and here's what's broken."* The M1 confidence was Medium because the data story hadn't been told loudly enough. The current confidence is also Medium, but for a sharper reason: Loop 1 is broken across three modules, and we know it. That self-awareness is what makes the bet credible. We're not pretending the system compounds when it doesn't. We're naming the gap and showing the plan to close it.

*From Liquid Content as the headline to Liquid Content as one feature inside HQ.* The original bet rested on multi-format generation. The HQ bet rests on three pillars sharing signal. Multi-format generation is now a capability inside the AI Content Assistant inside the Communication & Engagement pillar. Same product, much larger context.
